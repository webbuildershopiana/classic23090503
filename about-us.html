TEA HISTORY IN INDIA
Know about the tea history
Tea has a rich history in India, dating back to the ancient times when it was used for medicinal purposes. The British East India Company played a pivotal role in commercializing tea cultivation in India during the 19th century. In 1823, the first tea plantation was established in Assam, marking the birth of the Indian tea industry. Assam and Darjeeling became renowned for their distinctive tea varieties. The Indian tea industry faced challenges, such as labor issues and competition, but it grew steadily. India is now one of the world's largest tea producers and consumers, with diverse tea types, including black, green, and chai, enjoyed both domestically and internationally.
