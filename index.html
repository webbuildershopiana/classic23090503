TEA HISTORY IN INDIA
Know about the tea history
Tea has a rich history in India, dating back to the ancient times when it was used for medicinal purposes. The British East India Company played a pivotal role in commercializing tea cultivation in India during the 19th century. In 1823, the first tea plantation was established in Assam, marking the birth of the Indian tea industry. Assam and Darjeeling became renowned for their distinctive tea varieties. The Indian tea industry faced challenges, such as labor issues and competition, but it grew steadily. India is now one of the world's largest tea producers and consumers, with diverse tea types, including black, green, and chai, enjoyed both domestically and internationally.
TEA HARVESTING PROCESS
The tea harvesting process in Assam, India, involves several meticulous steps that ensures the quality and flavor of the region's renowned Assam tea. The process typically follows these key stages:
STEP 01
Plucking
Skilled pluckers select prime Assam leaves, early morning freshness.
STEP 02
Withering
Freshly plucked leaves wither, reduce moisture, enhance pliability.
STEP 03
Rolling
Rolled leaves break cells, start oxidation, boost flavor development.
STEP 04
Oxidation
Rolled leaves oxidize, create color, flavor; varies by tea type.
STEP 05
Fermentation
Oxidized leaves, called 'fermented,' deepen flavor, aroma, and color.
STEP 06
Drying
Heat stops oxidation, preserving tea's achieved qualities.
STEP 07
Sorting
Sorted teas by size, shape, quality; grades define value.
STEP 08
Packaging
Packaged as loose leaves, tea bags, or specialty forms worldwide.
